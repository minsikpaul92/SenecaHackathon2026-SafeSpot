Many communities rely on public facilities like libraries, schools, and community centers as warming or cooling spaces during extreme weather. Utilities and municipalities need simple ways to visualize where climate risks and limited access to safe shelter spaces overlap.Solution: A conceptual web app that helps Toronto communities prepare for extreme weather by combining real-time temperature sensor data, urban heat island mapping, and smart routing to the nearest cooling centre or library.TeamNameRoleGaryHardware — Raspberry Pi & Temperature SensorMarcosBackend — FastAPI (Python)PaulFrontend — Next.js, Leaflet.js Map IntegrationSeulgiFrontend — UI/UX Design, Alert SystemArunFrontend — GPS Location, Nearest Shelter RoutingTech StackLayerTechnologyFrontendNext.js, Leaflet.jsBackendFastAPI (Python)HardwareRaspberry Pi + Temperature SensorData SourcesArcGIS REST API (Urban Heat Island, Cooling Centres, Libraries)Map LibraryLeaflet.js with GeoJSON layersCore Features1.
